MVP Rules Matrix
Rule 001

Topic: Initial claim main form
Official rule: Use VA Form 21-526EZ for disability compensation and related benefits.
Applies when: User is starting a new disability compensation claim.
User trigger: “I want to file a new claim.”
Copilot action: Surface 21-526EZ as the likely primary form.
Warning: Do not imply this is the only possible supporting form.
Source: About VA Form 21-526EZ; How to File a VA Disability Claim.

Rule 002

Topic: Initial claim evidence
Official rule: Initial claims may require DD214/separation documents, service treatment records, and medical evidence related to the condition.
Applies when: User is preparing an initial disability claim.
User trigger: “What do I need?”
Copilot action: Generate evidence checklist.
Warning: Phrase as “may need” unless the evidence requirement is explicit.
Source: Evidence Needed for Your Disability Claim.

Rule 003

Topic: Lay evidence
Official rule: VA may accept lay evidence, including buddy statements, in some circumstances.
Applies when: User lacks records or wants to support symptoms/events through witness statements.
User trigger: “Can my spouse/friend write something?”
Copilot action: Mention lay evidence and possible use of VA Form 21-10210.
Warning: Do not overstate lay evidence as a substitute for all medical evidence.
Source: Evidence Needed for Your Disability Claim.

Rule 004

Topic: Intent to file
Official rule: Intent to file can preserve a potential effective date and gives the claimant one year to complete and file the claim.
Applies when: User is not ready to file but wants to preserve timing.
User trigger: “I’m not ready yet.”
Copilot action: Suggest learning about intent to file.
Warning: Do not guarantee effective date outcomes.
Source: Your Intent to File a VA Claim.

Rule 005

Topic: Claim stages
Official rule: VA disability claims move through stages like initial review, evidence gathering, evidence review, rating, and decision.
Applies when: User asks where they are in the process or what happens next.
User trigger: “What stage am I in?”
Copilot action: Explain stages in plain language.
Warning: Status can move backward if more evidence is needed or submitted.
Source: The VA Claim Process After You File Your Claim; What Your Claim Status Means.

Rule 006

Topic: Supplemental Claim
Official rule: Supplemental Claims are for adding new and relevant evidence to a case.
Applies when: User disagrees with a decision and has evidence not previously considered.
User trigger: “I got denied and now I have more evidence.”
Copilot action: Route toward Supplemental Claim explanation.
Warning: Clarify “new” and “relevant.”
Source: Supplemental Claims; 38 CFR 3.2501.

Rule 007

Topic: Higher-Level Review
Official rule: Higher-Level Review is for review of the existing record and does not permit new evidence.
Applies when: User thinks VA made an error and has no new evidence.
User trigger: “They missed something in my file.”
Copilot action: Route toward HLR explanation.
Warning: If the user says they have new evidence, HLR should be discouraged.
Source: Higher-Level Reviews; Decision Reviews FAQs.

Rule 008

Topic: HLR informal conference
Official rule: Informal conference is optional, and VA may decide the case without one if contact does not occur.
Applies when: User asks whether to request a call.
User trigger: “Should I ask for an informal conference?”
Copilot action: Explain it as optional and suggest concise issue framing.
Warning: Do not imply the conference guarantees a favorable result.
Source: Higher-Level Reviews.

Rule 009

Topic: Board Appeal
Official rule: Board Appeals include Direct Review, Evidence Submission, and Hearing lanes, each with different evidence/hearing rules.
Applies when: User wants a judge review or is beyond initial review options.
User trigger: “I want to appeal to a judge.”
Copilot action: Provide high-level Board explainer.
Warning: Do not fully automate Board strategy in MVP.
Source: Board Appeals.

Rule 010

Topic: Review deadlines
Official rule: For most VA benefits, users generally have one year from the decision letter date to request Higher-Level Review or Board Appeal; Supplemental Claims can be filed later, but filing within one year may matter for preserving timing.
Applies when: User mentions a prior decision.
User trigger: “I got a decision months ago.”
Copilot action: Ask for decision date and warn about timing.
Warning: Do not promise preserved effective date in every scenario.
Source: Decision Reviews FAQs.

Rule 011

Topic: No concurrent review misuse
Official rule: Review pathways are constrained; users should not be guided as though they can freely pursue all lanes at once.
Applies when: User wants to “do everything at once.”
User trigger: “Can I file HLR and Board Appeal together?”
Copilot action: Warn that the review system has structured lane rules.
Warning: Keep the explanation simple in MVP.
Source: 38 CFR 3.2500; Choosing a Decision Review Option.

Rule 012

Topic: Accredited help
Official rule: Accredited attorneys, agents, and VSO reps can help file claims and request decision reviews; accreditation rules matter.
Applies when: User asks for direct advice, representation, or filing help.
User trigger: “Can you tell me exactly what to file?”
Copilot action: Provide educational guidance only and escalate to accredited help.
Warning: Never present the app as a representative.
Source: Get Help from a VA Accredited Representative; OGC Accreditation FAQs.

Rule 013

Topic: Evidence upload friction
Official rule: QuickSubmit is an official evidence intake path and tracks uploads.
Applies when: User asks how to upload documents or worries whether something was received.
User trigger: “How do I send documents?”
Copilot action: Mention official upload path and encourage proof of submission.
Warning: Do not imply the app itself sends documents to VA.
Source: QuickSubmit is the new evidence intake tool for VA claims.

Rule 014

Topic: Community friction handling
Official rule: Community claims are not authoritative.
Applies when: You store or surface veteran-reported tips.
User trigger: Internal product behavior only.
Copilot action: Label community advice as anecdotal UX guidance only.
Warning: Never let a Reddit tip override an official rule.
Source: Product design rule derived from official-vs-community distinction. Supported by official VA pathways and community friction examples.
